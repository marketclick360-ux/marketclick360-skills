# Stage 1 — Extract Patterns from ONE Interview

## Purpose

Read the private source material for a **single** interview and extract only
**broad, non-identifying patterns**. You are building an abstract pattern map,
not a summary of a person. If a detail could help someone recognize this
individual, it does not belong in your output.

## Ground rules (non-negotiable)

- The source text is **private working material**. Do not quote it. Do not
  closely paraphrase it. Do not reproduce its memorable phrasing.
- Treat every `[FLAG:...]` span as identifying material: **exclude it** or
  replace it with a broad category (e.g. `[FLAG:Riverside Motel]` → "low-cost
  temporary housing").
- **When in doubt, generalize or exclude.** Losing a detail is acceptable;
  leaking one is not.

## Prohibited in your output

- Direct quotes of any length
- Close paraphrases or distinctive turns of phrase
- Exact ages (say "adolescence", "early adulthood", "midlife" instead)
- Exact places (say "a rural area", "a large city" instead)
- Exact dates or years (say "roughly a decade ago", "during childhood" instead)
- Unique incidents that would be recognizable to anyone who knows the story
- Rare combinations of details (an unusual job + an unusual family structure
  is identifying even if each alone is not)
- Sensationalized or voyeuristic framing — describe hardship plainly and
  respectfully

## Produce a markdown pattern map with EXACTLY these headings

### 1. Broad Background Patterns
General life-context patterns (e.g. "grew up with economic instability"),
each stated at a level that would fit hundreds of people.

### 2. Family / Social Context
Generalized relational patterns: caregiver presence/absence, household
stability, peer influences — no family compositions rare enough to identify.

### 3. Economic Pressures
Patterns of financial strain, work instability, housing insecurity — stated
categorically, never with amounts, employers, or specific jobs if rare.

### 4. Adversity Themes
Broad categories of hardship (e.g. "exposure to substance abuse in the
home"). Never reproduce the specific traumatic incident; name only the theme.

### 5. Coping / Survival Strategies
How the person managed: generalized behaviors, supports used or missing.

### 6. Emotional Patterns
Recurring emotional states or arcs expressed across the interview.

### 7. Turning Points
Types of turning points (e.g. "an institutional intervention", "a
relationship ending") — the category, never the recognizable event itself.

### 8. Systemic Factors
Broader forces at play: healthcare access, housing policy, justice system
contact, labor markets — described generically.

### 9. Excluded Identifying Details (log only, do not describe precisely)
A category-level list of what you deliberately left out, e.g. "a specific
city name", "an exact age", "a distinctive workplace incident". Do NOT record
the details themselves.

### 10. Uncertainty Notes
Where the source was ambiguous, where you inferred, and where confidence is
low. Be honest about gaps rather than filling them in.

## Output format

Markdown only, with the ten headings above. No preamble, no source text
echoed back.
