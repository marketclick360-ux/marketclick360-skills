# Stage 2 — Cross-Interview Synthesis

## Purpose

Compare the Stage 1 pattern maps from **multiple separate interviews** and
identify themes that genuinely recur across them. The output of this stage is
the ONLY permitted raw material for composite generation, so its job is to
guarantee that nothing traceable to a single person survives.

## The minimum recurrence rule (hard requirement)

**A trait, theme, or pattern may be included ONLY if it appears in at least
3 separate interviews.** Two is not enough. A vivid pattern from one
interview is exactly the kind of detail that re-identifies someone — exclude
it, no matter how compelling.

If fewer than 3 pattern maps were provided, say so explicitly and refuse to
produce an includable-themes table.

## Additional rules

- One-off details are excluded, always — log them (category only) instead.
- Do not merge two different rare details into a "shared" theme; a theme is
  shared only when the SAME generalized pattern appears independently.
- Never carry forward quotes, close paraphrases, exact ages/places/dates, or
  `[FLAG:...]` content, even if it appears in multiple maps.
- When in doubt, generalize further or exclude.

## Produce this output

### A. Recurring Themes Table

A markdown table with these columns:

| Theme | Generalized description | Interviews it appears in (count) | Recurrence confidence |
|---|---|---|---|

Rules for the table:
- Only themes meeting the 3-interview minimum appear here.
- **Recurrence confidence labels:**
  - **High** — clearly present in 5+ interviews, or in nearly all of a small set
  - **Medium** — clearly present in 3–4 interviews
  - **Low** — present in 3 interviews but with interpretive uncertainty
- Count interviews, not mentions: a theme repeated ten times in one
  interview still counts as one interview.

### B. Theme Clusters

Group the included themes into 3–6 named clusters (e.g. "economic
instability", "institutional contact", "relational rupture and repair") with
a sentence describing each cluster.

### C. Excluded Detail Log

A category-level list of patterns that were considered and excluded, with the
reason: "appeared in fewer than 3 interviews", "too specific even when
generalized", "rare-detail combination risk". Do NOT record the underlying
details themselves.

### D. Anonymization Guidance for Stage 3

Concrete instructions for the composite writer: which themes are safe to
build on, which clusters need extra generalization, what categories of
detail must be invented as fiction rather than drawn from sources, and any
set-specific risks (e.g. "this interview set skews toward one region — do
not let regional cues through").

## Output format

Markdown: the table in section A, then sections B–D as headed notes.
