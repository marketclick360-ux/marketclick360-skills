# Stage 3 — Composite Generation

## Purpose

Write a **fictionalized, anonymized composite profile** built ONLY from the
recurring cross-interview themes in the Stage 2 synthesis map. The composite
is a fictional person who illustrates patterns shared by many people. It must
never be a disguised summary of any real individual.

## Hard requirements

- **Length:** 500–900 words for the profile itself (Section A).
- **Tone:** documentary-style — grounded, specific about patterns, plain
  about hardship. Humane and non-voyeuristic: no sensationalism, no
  trauma-as-spectacle, no pity language. Write about a person, not a case.
- **Source discipline:** every source-derived element must come from a theme
  that met the 3-interview minimum in Stage 2. Nothing from a single
  interview may appear, even generalized.
- **Fiction discipline:** everything that makes the profile feel like one
  coherent life — the name-free persona, the sequence of events, the
  connective scenes — must be invented, and must not echo any source.

## Prohibited

- Direct quotes from any source material
- Close paraphrases or distinctive phrasing from any interview
- Unique biographical details from any single interview
- Exact ages, dates, years, or locations (use "in adolescence", "a
  mid-sized industrial city", "years later")
- Real names, or names similar to any source's name
- Any claim or implication that this is a real person, or that any single
  event described actually happened to someone

## Required label

The output MUST begin with this exact line:

> **Fictionalized anonymized composite.** This profile does not describe a
> real person. It is a fictional illustration of patterns that recurred
> across multiple interviews.

## Produce EXACTLY these five sections

### A. Composite Profile
The 500–900 word documentary-style profile, beginning with the required
label above.

### B. Source-Derived Pattern Basis
A bulleted list of every recurring theme used, each with its Stage 2
recurrence confidence (High/Medium/Low). This is the audit trail proving the
profile is pattern-based.

### C. Fictionalized Connective Tissue
A bulleted list of the invented elements — the fictional scenes, sequence,
persona traits, and transitions you created to make the patterns read as one
life. Everything not in Section B must be listed here.

### D. Details Intentionally Excluded
Category-level list of the kinds of details you kept out (exact places,
ages, unique incidents, rare trait combinations) and why.

### E. Re-identification Risk Notes
Your own honest assessment: which parts of the profile carry the most
residual risk, and what a Stage 4 reviewer should scrutinize first.

## Final self-check before you answer

Ask yourself: if a person who appeared in one of the source interviews read
this profile, could they reasonably say "that's me"? If yes for any single
source, generalize further and rewrite before responding.
