# Domain Context (optional — edit or delete for other transcript sets)

If this file exists, `build_prompt_packets.py` embeds it in every prompt
packet so all four stages share the same domain framing. Replace its
contents when you point the workflow at a different interview set.

## Current domain profile

**Source material:** Long-form one-on-one interviews in the style of the
Soft White Underbelly channel — people discussing difficult life
experiences: marginalization, addiction, trauma, survival, family
instability, poverty, sex work, incarceration, homelessness, and recovery.

**Analytical goal:** Extract recurring social, emotional, and systemic
patterns across interviews and express them only as fictionalized,
anonymized composite examples for analysis and writing.

## Theme areas to watch for (pattern level only)

- Addiction and recovery arcs (onset context, relapse pressures, what
  helped)
- Housing instability and homelessness pathways
- Family instability: caregiver absence, foster/kinship care, early
  responsibility
- Poverty and economic survival strategies
- Sex work as economic survival, and its associated risks and stigma
- Incarceration and justice-system contact, and re-entry barriers
- Trauma exposure and its long-tail effects (named as themes, never as
  incidents)
- Marginalization and stigma: how institutions and communities responded
- Resilience, turning points, and recovery supports

## Domain-specific identifier risks (treat ALL of these as identifying)

This population is unusually re-identifiable. Beyond the standard rules,
generalize or exclude:

- **Street/stage names and aliases** — for many interviewees an alias IS
  their identity; treat it exactly like a legal name.
- **Named facilities** — specific jails, prisons, rehabs, shelters,
  hospitals, motels ("a state prison", "a residential program" instead).
- **Neighborhood, corner, strip, or block references** — famous street
  names and districts are as identifying as a full address.
- **Gang or crew affiliations** — name the category ("gang involvement"),
  never the group.
- **Specific charges, sentences, or case details** — "justice-system
  contact in adulthood", not the charge or the years.
- **Specific substances tied to a distinctive personal arc** — a common
  substance is usually safe at pattern level; an unusual combination or
  signature detail is not.
- **Distinctive physical details** — tattoos, scars, injuries, or medical
  conditions memorable enough to recognize.
- **Recognizable story beats** — these interviews are public and widely
  viewed; a vivid incident from one interview is traceable even with every
  name removed. The 3-interview recurrence rule exists precisely for this.

## Tone requirements for this domain

Composites must treat their subjects with dignity: documentary plainness,
no voyeurism, no trauma-as-spectacle, no moralizing. The point is to
illuminate recurring social and systemic patterns — not to retell anyone's
worst day.
