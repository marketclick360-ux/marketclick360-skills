# Content Formats & Anonymization Rules

## LinkedIn formats (rotate)

1. **Teardown** — "How an AI employee handles {workflow}, step by step"
2. **Before/after** — "{X} hrs/week → {Y} min of human review. What changed:"
3. **Lesson from failure** — "An agent failed in shadow week this week. Here's
   the failure and the fix." (Failure content outperforms success content for
   authority.)
4. **Operator POV** — an opinion on agents/business, earned from delivery
5. **Numbers post** — monthly anonymized portfolio stats

## Weekly pillar workflow

Wednesday block: mine artifacts → 2–3 angles proposed → operator picks + records
raw take → draft pillar + posts → anonymization pass → operator edit (45 min)
→ schedule.

## Anonymization rules (existential — a client recognizing themselves ends the relationship)

- No client names, ever
- No identifying industry + geography combos: "a Tampa roofing company" → "a
  home-services business"
- Round all numbers to bands; no unique figures traceable to one company
- Composite details when a story is too identifiable
- When in doubt, flag to the operator — never decide "it's probably fine"

## Case-study pipeline

Every client's week-12 value summary is a candidate → ask permission at the
renewal high point → signed release → agent drafts long-form from real metrics
→ becomes proposal proof and pillar content. Target: 1 named case study per
quarter.
