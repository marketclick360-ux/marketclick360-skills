# Client Communication Templates

Fill `{PLACEHOLDERS}` from the client vault. Tone: warm, concise, premium-but-plain.
No jargon, no hype. All sends require operator approval.

## Post-discovery / intake invite
> Great speaking today. Next step: our intake diagnostic (~25 min) — it's how we
> design an AI employee around how your business actually runs, not a generic
> bot. Link: {LINK}. Once it's in, you'll have a proposal within {N} business days.

## Proposal cover
> Attached is your proposal. Page 1 is the AI employee's job description — the
> specific work it takes off your plate. We deliberately kept scope tight for
> v1; the monthly improvement cycle is where it grows. Valid for 14 days;
> onboarding slots are limited to {N}/month.

## Kickoff recap + access checklist
> Kickoff recap + your access checklist below. Each item takes ~2 minutes and
> includes exact steps. Once access is in, your AI employee goes into training
> ("shadow week") — you'll get one note when training starts and one when it
> goes live. Nothing reaches your customers during training.

## Shadow-week notice
> Your AI employee is in training this week — running on real work, supervised,
> with nothing going out. We use this week to correct its judgment before it
> acts on your behalf. Go-live: {DATE}.

## Go-live announcement
> {NAME}, your AI employee is live. As of today it handles: {WORKFLOWS}. You'll
> get a short report every Monday showing exactly what it did. Anything ever
> looks off — reply here; a monitored system alerts me before most issues are
> visible, but you always have a direct line.

## Out-of-scope request (scope-creep defense)
> Great idea — that's beyond the current tier's scope, so I've added it to your
> improvement backlog. It'd be included at {NEXT_TIER}, or we can swap it for
> {CURRENT_BACKLOG_ITEM} in next month's improvement cycle.
