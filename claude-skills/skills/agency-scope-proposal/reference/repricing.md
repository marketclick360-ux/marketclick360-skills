# Repricing & Renewal Playbook

## Triggers (check monthly per client)

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Backlog holds 3+ approved-value requests beyond tier | Upgrade conversation, backlog as evidence |
| Utilization >130% of tier for 2 consecutive months | Upgrade or trim scope — never silently absorb |
| Documented value ≥5× retainer for a quarter | Reprice at renewal with the value summary |
| Operator hours on client >8/mo for 2 months | Up-tier or off-board; no exceptions |
| Client requests meeting cadence | Cadence lives at the $8k tier |

## Value summary one-pager (renewal evidence pack)

Assembled from 8–12 weeks of metrics:
- Cumulative work delivered (counts by workflow)
- Estimated $ impact — computed ONLY with the client's own unit economics from
  intake (lead value, customer value), source cited per number
- Tier utilization vs. plan
- Backlog contents (demand beyond the tier)
- Tier recommendation + 5 talking points

Rule: understate rather than overstate. Every number must survive the client
checking it.

## Upgrade pitch template

> In the last {N} weeks your AI employee delivered {VALUE}. Your backlog now
> has {ITEMS} beyond the current tier. At {NEXT_TIER} we'd cover those plus
> {BENEFIT}. Same-tier renewal is fine too — but the backlog suggests demand
> has outgrown the plan.

## Scope-creep reply template

> Great idea — that's beyond the current tier's scope, so I've added it to your
> improvement backlog. It'd be included at {NEXT_TIER}, or we can swap it for
> {CURRENT_BACKLOG_ITEM} in next month's improvement cycle.
